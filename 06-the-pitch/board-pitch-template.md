# Board Pitch

> **Purpose.** The 1-page pitch a board member (or investor, or executive sponsor) can read in 90 seconds and walk away knowing what you're building, why now, what's defensible, what the risks are, what you're asking for. Not a deck. Not a doc. A page.
>
> **The structural insight this doc serves.** Strategy docs are for the operator. The pitch is for the person who decides whether you keep operating. They are not the same audience and the pitch is not "the strategy doc shortened" — it is its own artifact, written *for the decision the audience will make*. Every other module in this repo earns the right to one or two sentences here. If a module's finding doesn't make it into the pitch, either the finding is weak or the pitch is.
>
> **How to use it.** Write the thesis last, even though it reads first. Most teams write the thesis first and then bend the case to fit it — the result is a pitch that doesn't survive its own scrutiny.

## Thesis (1 sentence)

> *One sentence. What the product is, who it's for, why it's defensible. If it takes two sentences, the bet is not yet sharp.*

---

## The case

### 1. Why now
*What changed in the last 12 months that makes this the moment? Model capability? Cost curve? Regulation? Distribution? If "why now" is weak, the bet is a wish.*

### 2. What's defensible
*What is the moat at month 24 that isn't there at month 1? Cite the data flywheel or compounding loop that builds it. "Brand" and "execution" are not moats.*

### 3. The economics
*Unit economics in one paragraph. Cost structure, pricing posture, what break-even looks like. Cite cost-curve and pricing-strategy findings.*
